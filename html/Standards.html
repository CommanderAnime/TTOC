Competition Standards

To keep the Competition fun and fair for everyone, all players and spectators must follow these standards accordingly based on their role in the competition.

In addition to these standards, the TankTrouble Community Standards must be followed throughout the Online Chat and the Forum at all times.

  1. Players must have at least 3 months of active participation in the TankTrouble Community, and have at least 5000 wins to sign up and compete.

  2. Players suspected of sharing accounts or entering more than once with alternate accounts will not be allowed to participate in TTOC. Only one tank per person, per year.

  3. Every match is to be watched by a designated Spectator, who must have at least 3 months of active participation in the TankTrouble Community, with no wins required, and must sign up to spectate through the Sign Up form.

  4. Match times will be organised by the TTOC Crew for all Players and Spectators based on their availability, and will be posted in UTC time.

  5. Players and Spectators must confirm or cancel their match no later than 12 hours before their match, otherwise they will be eliminated, and will no longer be allowed to participate in TTOC.

  6. The server listed beside each match is the meetup server for the Players and the Spectator. To change the match server, both players must agree and notify the Spectator at the meetup.

  7. The TankTrouble Standards apply at all times, and the Gamemode-specific Rules apply during all matches.

  8. If a Player or Spectator is absent for more than 10 total minutes anytime during the match, they will be eliminated, and will no longer be allowed to participate in TTOC.

  9. If the Spectator does not show up or must leave during the match, Players may either:
    • Continue the match on their own, with an extra tank signed in by each player. The final score must be confirmed by both Players in the Match thread.
    • Find a new Spectator that is qualified and has signed up to spectate.

  10. Spectators will keep score in chat during matches, and will keep 2 tanks signed in to block public spectators from entering and interfering with the match.

  11. If a gamemode rule is broken during the match, the player will receive a strike. After three strikes, each infraction will result in a point for the opponent.

  12. Complaints about lag and other uncontrollable factors or issues, as well as match interference, will not be tolerated.

  13. Final match scores, eliminations, and spectator switches must be reported by the match spectator on the Match Thread as soon as possible.

  14. Prizes are awarded to the player in a prize position, and may not be traded for an alternative or given to another player.
