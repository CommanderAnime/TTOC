Competition Standards

To keep the Competition fun and fair for everyone, all players and spectators must follow standards according to their role in the competition.

In addition to these standards, the TankTrouble Community Standards must be followed throughout the Online Chat and the Forum at all times.

View Spectator Standards

Player Standards

  1. Players must have at least 1 month of active participation in the TankTrouble Community and have at least 3000 wins to enter.

  2. The TankTrouble Standards apply at all times, and the Gamemode-specific Rules apply during all matches.

  3. Match times will be organised by the TTOC Crew for all players based on their availability and will be posted in UTC time.

  4. Players must confirm or cancel their match no later than 12 hours before their match.

  5. The server listed beside each match is the meetup server for the players and the spectator. If a player would like to change the match server, their opponent must agree and notify the spectator at the meetup.

  6. Players may only use keyboard controls and will be asked to perform the Twist (Hold down a turn control, while switching between pressing forward and backward control) at the start of the match to verify.

  7. If a player is absent for more than 10 total minutes anytime from the start until the end of the match, they will be eliminated, will no longer be allowed to participate in the rest of TTOC, and will not receive the TTOC tankcessory.

  8. If a gamemode rule is broken during a round, the opponent is awarded a win point.

  9. All matches begin when Spectator tanks are destroyed, except in the case of Gold Rush and Weapons. (See gamemode rules )

  10. To win a match, a player must have at least 15 wins with a 2-win difference, except in the case of Gold Rush. (See gamemode rules )

  11. If the Spectator does not show up, players may choose to play the match, with an extra tank signed in each. The final score must be posted and confirmed by both players on the Forum.

  12. Complaints about lag and other uncontrollable factors or issues, as well as match interference will not be tolerated.

  13. Prizes are awarded to the player in a prize position, and may not be traded for an alternative or given to another player.

  14. Players suspected of sharing accounts or entering more than once with alternate accounts will be permanently banned from the competition.

Spectator Standards

  1. Spectators must have at least 3 months of active participation in the TankTrouble Community, and must sign up to spectate through the Sign Up form.

  2. Spectators must enforce and follow the TankTrouble Standards , the Gamemode-specific Rules and the Player Standards during all matches.

  3. Spectators will be assigned to spectate matches by the TTOC Crew, based on their availability.

  4. If a spectator misses an assigned match, they will no longer be allowed to participate in the rest of TTOC, and will not receive the TTOC tankcessory.

  5. If a spectator must leave during a match, they must make sure that either the players are self-spectating, or find a qualified registered replacement from the Spectator list.

  6. Before starting the match, clarify any confusion between players.

  7. Before every match, or when a player returns to their match, ask them to perform the Twist (Hold down a turn control, while switching between pressing forward and backward control) to verify the use of keyboard controls.

  8. Report exact scores and the winner on the match thread after the match is complete.

  9. Spectators are allowed to leave and switch during matches if needed. Announce the switch immediately on the match thread.

  10. During matches, keep 2 tanks signed in to block unofficial spectators from entering and interfering with the match.

  11. If a player is absent for more than 10 total minutes anytime from the start until the end of the match, announce their elimination in the match thread.

  12. Address player concerns respectively. If a player misbehaves, announce their elimination in the match thread after one warning.

  13. Spectators must communicate with players at all times during a match, and maintain a professional attitude.
